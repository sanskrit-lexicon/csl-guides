# Borooah, *English-Sanskrit Dictionary* (`BOR`)

`BOR` is Anundoram Borooah's *English-Sanskrit Dictionary* (1877) — a substantial
**English→Sanskrit** dictionary by the pioneering Assamese scholar, giving Sanskrit equivalents
and illustrative sentences for English headwords. A tool for **composing** Sanskrit.

## When to use it

Reach for `BOR` to render **English into Sanskrit**, with example sentences. It complements
**[Apte's English-Sanskrit (AE)](ae)** — two independent English→Sanskrit dictionaries. For
reading Sanskrit, use **[MW](mw)** or **[Apte (AP90)](ap90)**.

## Looking up a word

Open the **Basic** display, type an **English** headword, and choose your output script for the
Sanskrit (see [Encoding & Transliteration](../users/encoding-transliteration)). See
[Search & Display](../users/search-and-display).

## Reading an entry

`BOR` sets the English headword in `{@…@}`, divides senses with `<div>`, and gives illustrative
phrases in `{%…%}`. The entry *A* (`csl-orig/v02/bor/bor.txt`):

```
<L>1<pc>001<k1>a<k2>a
{@A@}¦ {%or%} (before vowels) AN: <div n="I">I This article
has no equiv. in Sanskrit and is expressed
by the singular number alone: <div n="xe">{%there is a%}
{%large silk-cotton tree on the banks of the Godavari%}</div>
```

| In the source | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `{@A@}` | the **English** headword |
| `<div n="I">` | a sense division |
| `<div n="xe">` | an example (the English of which is given, with its Sanskrit rendering) |
| `{%…%}` | the illustrative phrase |

See [Data Formats](../developers/data-formats) for the markup reference.

## What makes it distinctive

- **English → Sanskrit, with examples.** Sanskrit equivalents plus illustrative sentences.
- **A landmark Indian-authored dictionary (1877).** By Anundoram Borooah.


## Headword key conventions

`<k1>` = `<k2>` = **English** word (the search key). Sanskrit equivalents and illustrative phrases appear in the entry body. A major English→Sanskrit dictionary by an Indian scholar.

| Convention | Marker / form | Example | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Search key | English word | `a` | Type English to find the entry; not SLP1 |
| Sense divisions | `<div n="I">`, `<div n="II">` | `<div n="I">I …` | Roman-numeral sense groups |
| Example phrase | `<div n="xe">` | marks an illustrative sentence | Followed by the English phrase in `{%…%}` |
| English phrase | `{%…%}` | `{%there is a silk-cotton tree%}` | Italic; the English phrase to be rendered in Sanskrit |
